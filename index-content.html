Alarm systems and specific alarms are needed for doors and windows: the US Department of Justice reports that hundreds of thousands of home break-ins happen each year, only a third of those happening through the front door. Others take place at other points of entry so protecting all your entries is paramount to you and your family's safety. Remember to consider glass break coverage in addition to contact sensors as robbers will try and enter in any way and might rely on a lack of glassbreaking coverage as their way in.

{Question Heading}

The architecture, groundplan, and landscaping of your home will all influence where motion detectors should be placed. Strategically laid out, these motion detectors will be key in protecting your home and offering a form of invisible security: intruders won't necessarily see the technology and are more likely to be ensnared by it.
