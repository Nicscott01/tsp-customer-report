Alarming Your Doors and Windows

It is good that you are taking proactive steps to protect your home by having windows and doors alarmed. Remember that contact alarms are helpful, but intruders will often not 'open' the door and window.  If you do not have a glass break alarm sensor, and the intruder accesses your property in that manner, your alarm will not go off. Glass break could be an important component to consider.

Motion Detectors

Motion detectors are not just helpful additions but are vital elements in securing your home. Mapping out where they track motion, especially in more concealed places, further reinforces your home's security.

Monitored Alarms

Monitored alarms, depending on how long ago you got yours, have evolved significantly over the past few years. Now you can have access to your home's security system all from your smartphone. Keep your notifications turned on and regularly update your app to ensure it is performing at its best for you.

No matter your type of home, there are a number of tips that help keep your house safe and your loved ones protected:

*Alarm All Entry Points: It's not just doors that should be alarmed — windows should be as well. Especially on the first floor, burglars may try and bypass doors knowing they're alarmed. Remember to consider glass break solution as well as motion sensors in key inside areas.

*Alarm systems are like our bodies: they are built to protect us and now and then need routine check-ups. Take good care of your house by getting an alarm system diagnosis; see which areas need surveillance, which doors are easy to break into, and which entry points have gone forgotten.

*Light your home at night: An often-forgotten technique, some simple indoor (even timed) lighting as well as strategically placed outdoor lighting can be highly effective in keeping intruders away. Lighting exposes intruders and tells them that someone is home and/or that your property is going to be too difficult to penetrate without discovery.

*Have a professional inspection: Maybe you installed your alarm system yourself or feel comfortable with the amount of security you have, but a professional inspector will be able to notify you of at-risk spots on your home, where to place cameras and more.

*Ensure Your Safety:  Invest in backup power (a generator) in case of power failure. You should also have your system inspected to ensure maintenance, especially if you change the architecture or layout of a home, which can impact sightlines and camera angles.
